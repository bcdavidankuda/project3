Minsk, Belarus

Welcome to Minsk

Minsk, the capital city of Belarus, offers a unique blend of history, culture, and modernity. It's a city that attracts visitors with its stunning architecture, vibrant arts scene, and rich history. Whether you're interested in exploring historical sites, enjoying local cuisine, or experiencing the lively nightlife, Minsk has something for everyone.

Top Attractions

Independence Square

Independence Square is one of the largest squares in Europe. It features the iconic Independence Palace and the Church of Saints Simon and Helena. Learn more

National Library of Belarus

The National Library of Belarus is an architectural marvel and a treasure trove of knowledge. Its unique diamond-shaped design houses millions of books and offers panoramic views of the city from its observation deck. Learn more

Victory Park

Victory Park is a beautiful green space dedicated to the heroes of World War II. It includes a museum, monuments, and a serene lake. Learn more

Belarusian State Museum of the History of the Great Patriotic War

This museum offers a comprehensive look at Belarus's role in World War II, with numerous exhibits and artifacts. Learn more

Interesting Facts

  • Population: Approximately 2 million
  • Geography: Located on the Svislach and Niamiha rivers
  • Weather: Humid continental climate with cold winters and warm summers
  • Language: Belarusian and Russian
